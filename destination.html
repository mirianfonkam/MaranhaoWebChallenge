Destinations

Discover unforgettable sights.

Maranhão is home to beaches, forests, rivers and lakes that differentiate it from other Brazilian destinations. Its preserved area of forests, immaculate white sands and wonderful coastal scenery are majestic.

National Park of Lençóis Maranhenses

Maranhão's premier attraction, this 1550-sq-km national park encompasses expanses of white sand dunes that run along the coastline. Its landscapes are particularly impressive from May to September, when rain filtered through the sand forms crystal-clear pools and lakes between the dunes; these are fantastic for swimming.

Alcântara, Maranhão

Located on the continent and bathed by the ocean, Alcântara has beautiful secrets to be unveiled by the visitor. The rich set of historic mansions and ruins allow you to take a true journey into the past and imagine the customs of colonial times.

Morros, Maranhão

Characterized by a large number of hills, Morros has beautiful landscapes of native forest, covered by distinct plant formations. The municipality is part of the Munim basin and is bathed by the rivers Munim, Una and others. The main tourist attractions in Morros are the crystalline and cold rivers, as well as the churches and antique houses.

São José de Ribamar, Maranhão

São José de Ribamar is synonymous with peace and tranquility. The city is 32 kilometers from São Luís and has attractions such as beaches, delicious seafood and stories, revolving around the figure of the patron saint – São José. According to legend, the city's church has collapsed twice before it was built facing the sea, as was the wish of the Saint.

São Luís, Maranhão

The World Heritage-listed historical center of São Luís is an enchanting neighborhood of steamy cobbled streets and pastel-colored colonial mansions. It’s a charming area with a unique atmosphere and one of the Northeast's best concentrations of museums, galleries and craft stores.

The city as a whole has a markedly Afro-Brazilian tinge to its culture. The city is crowned capital of reggae in Brazil and is home to the highly colorful Bumba Meu Boi festivities.

National Park of Chapada das Mesas

One of Brazil's newest national parks, Parque Nacional da Chapada das Mesas is renowned for its dramatic flat-topped rocky bluffs ("mesas" means tables). It also boasts many beautiful waterfalls, canyons and crystalline swimming holes.
