Culture

Immerse yourself in a unique and vibrant experience.

In the cultural mix created by the presence of the Portuguese, Dutch, French, indigenous and Africans, Maranhão acquired exclusive cultural traits which are revealed in its gastronomy, dances and popular festivals.

Food

The cuisine of Maranhão is characterized by a distinct and relatively light seasoning, making use of ingredients such as chive, black pepper, seafood and cassava flour.

Açai, know here as "Juçara", is also highly appreciated by people from Maranhão, and is also consumed with cassava flour, shrimp, fish or dried meat.

The most popular soft drink in Maranhão is Guaraná Jesus. With the slogan "The taste of living Maranhão", the Guaraná Jesus is a mixture of spices such as cloves, cinnamon and tutti-frutti. It was acquired by Coca-Cola in 2001, but is still more widely marketed in the state.

Festivities & Dances

Dances influenced by African, indigenous and Portuguese cultures are always present in popular festivities and special events that take place throughout the state.

The bumba-meu-boi, present mainly during the period of June festivities, is however the trademark of popular festivities in Maranhão, bringing together elements of theatre, dance and music. There are more than a hundred bumba-meu-boi groups, which bring together communities and tourists, and spend the night during their performances.
